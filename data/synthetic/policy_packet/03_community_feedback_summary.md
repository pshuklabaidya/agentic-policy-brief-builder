# SYNTHETIC DOCUMENT: Community Feedback Summary

**Synthetic data notice:** This community feedback summary is synthetic. All comments, stakeholder categories, and figures are fictional and created only for a public demo.

## Topic

Local housing affordability and zoning reform in the fictional City of Riverton.

## Outreach Snapshot

The fictional Housing and Neighborhoods Office hosted four listening sessions, received 214 survey responses, and met with renters, homeowners, small landlords, disability advocates, transit riders, and local builders.

## Common Themes

- Renters emphasized the need for lower-cost apartments near jobs, schools, and bus routes.
- Homeowners asked for clear design standards, tree preservation, and predictable construction rules.
- Disability advocates requested accessible units, safe sidewalks, and continued accessible parking enforcement.
- Small builders said predictable approvals would reduce carrying costs and make smaller projects more feasible.
- Anti-displacement advocates supported new homes but requested monitoring for rent increases, eviction filings, and speculative property acquisitions.

## Areas Of Agreement

Most synthetic participants supported faster permitting for accessory dwelling units, clearer public dashboards, and annual reporting on whether zoning reform is improving affordability without concentrating impacts in a small number of neighborhoods.
